ABC7 Los Angeles

New food-industry grants open for Los Angeles restaurants and cafes

Published 1 day ago

Los Angeles restaurant and café owners have a new opportunity to secure financial support this year, thanks to a recently launched food-industry grant program offering up to $15,000 for equipment upgrades. The initiative aims to help small businesses modernize their kitchens, improve service efficiency, and keep pace with increasing customer demand.

According to ABC7 Los Angeles, the grant is open to independent restaurants, small cafés, food stands, coffee shops, and other licensed food-service businesses operating within Los Angeles County. Funds can be used to replace outdated equipment, purchase energy-efficient appliances, upgrade refrigeration, or invest in tools that support food safety and quality.

Local business owners say the grant comes at a critical time. Many restaurants continue to face rising costs for supplies, maintenance, and labor, making it difficult to reinvest in the equipment needed to stay competitive.

Applications for the program are expected to remain open until funding is exhausted. Business owners are encouraged to apply early and prepare documentation such as permits, financials, and a brief explanation of how the funds will be used.

More details and application links will be available through participating Los Angeles business-support organizations.
